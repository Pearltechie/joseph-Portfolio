Maji Ndogo: Data Analytics for Water Access

In many regions across Maji Ndogo, access to clean and reliable water remains a significant challenge. This data-driven project explores water distribution inefficiencies, long wait times, and corruption in supply chains. Using SQL, Power BI, and spreadsheet analysis, we identified key issues affecting water access and proposed actionable solutions for stakeholders.

Problem Statement

  • Millions of people in underserved regions face inconsistent water supply and long wait times at public taps.
  • Corruption in distribution networks leads to unfair allocation and inflated costs.
  • Lack of data transparency makes it difficult for policymakers to address these issues effectively.

Tools & Techniques

SQL Power BI Excel

Key Insights & Findings

  • Unequal Distribution: Certain regions had consistently lower access despite higher demand.
  • Corruption Detected: SQL analysis exposed suspicious transactions, such as repeated high-volume allocations.
  • Long Wait Times: On average, people spent 3-5 hours daily fetching water due to inefficiencies.
  • Data-Driven Recommendations: Suggested reallocation strategies and anti-corruption measures.

Impact & Proposed Solutions

  • Increased Transparency: The Dashboard helps track distribution in real time.
  • Optimized Supply Chains: Predictive modeling enables better resource allocation.
  • Policy Recommendations: Suggested anti-corruption audits and equitable water rationing.

Lessons Learned

  • Data analytics can expose hidden inefficiencies in essential services like water distribution.
  • Combining SQL and visualization tools improves decision-making for policy implementation.
  • Ethical considerations are critical when handling sensitive community data.
